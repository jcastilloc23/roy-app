★ Major Labels Skip the Distributor

Universal, Sony, and Warner have direct licensing agreements with every major DSP. Spotify pays them directly — no middleman, no fee. The label then pays artists their contractual royalty, only after recouping advances.

The Indie Advantage

Independent artists who own their masters and use a distribution deal (DistroKid, TuneCore, The Orchard) keep 80–90% of master royalties instead of 15–25%. The trade-off is no label advance and no major-label promotion machine.

The Songwriter Gap

Songwriters receive only ~9.4¢ per $1 of stream revenue. US terrestrial AM/FM radio pays nothing to the master recording side at all — a gap that barely exists anywhere else in the world.

Two Parallel Pipelines

Every stream triggers two completely separate royalty flows: the composition side (MLC + PROs → publisher → songwriter) and the master side (directly to the label, or via distributor for indie artists). Different rates, collectors, and timelines.
